Who are we?

We are campers at Math in the Mountains Beta Camp. Math in the Mountains is an outdoor math camp at TSS in Jackson Hole for math enthusiastic children, parents, and counselors. In addition to difficult math with speakers and teachers such as Ken Ono, Po-Shen Loh, and Paul Zeitz, MitM features thrilling outdoor activities such as rock climbing, ropes courses, and canoeing. Beta Camp is a pilot camp for students ages 12–14, which focuses on entrepreneurship, collaboration, and project creation.

What is our goal?

Our mission is to create an accessible, engaging, enjoyable math program to give everyone a good math education and community that they can rely on. We understand that many other online math programs can be hard to follow, aren't personalized enough, or aren't the right difficulty. We wanted to create a place where a learner can choose what order they learn in, which includes suggested prerequisites and big questions to better understand why the math matters.



What is Project1716?

Project1716 is a student created and curated math education platform to solve these problems. Project1716 features courses with curated videos, which include teaching, practice questions, and time for answering questions and making observations. It features visual elements such as trees and graphs to help understand the flow of the course. In addition, these elements will help the user understand why certain subjects matter and what questions they can be used to answer.
